# Hygiene

## Dental

### Mouthwash Cap
The cap on a mouthwash bottle serves a dual purpose most people overlook:
- The **inner cap** is sized for a single dose of mouthwash — fill it to the line and use that amount to rinse.
- The **outer cap** (the larger cup the inner cap sits inside) is meant for rinsing your mouth with water afterward.

### Listerine Access Flosser
A floss pick with a small reservoir that you can fill with mouthwash, letting you floss and rinse tight spaces simultaneously. Great for people who struggle to floss consistently.

### Burst USB-C Travel Toothbrush
The [Burst](https://www.burst.com) travel toothbrush charges via USB-C and comes with a protective cap that doubles as a travel case for the brush head. Keeps the bristles clean in your bag and is always ready to charge from the same cable as your phone.

## Body

### Lush Cosmetics
[Lush](https://www.lushusa.com) makes solid shampoo bars, conditioner bars, and body wash bars that are packaging-free. They last longer than bottled products, reduce plastic waste, and are TSA-friendly for carry-on travel.

### Dyson
[Dyson](https://www.dyson.com) makes hair care tools (Airwrap, Supersonic hair dryer) that use lower heat than traditional tools while delivering professional results, significantly reducing heat damage over time.

## Intimate Hygiene

### Cardboard Tampon Applicators
Cardboard applicators are biodegradable, reducing plastic waste compared to plastic applicators. Brands like Seventh Generation and Natracare offer these as a more sustainable alternative.

### Bidet Attachments
A bidet seat or attachment (e.g., [Tushy](https://hellotushy.com), [Brondell](https://www.brondell.com)) provides a more thorough and hygienic clean than toilet paper alone, reduces toilet paper consumption significantly, and can be installed on most standard toilets without plumbing changes.
